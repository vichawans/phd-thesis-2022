\section{Summary of key results}

The main aim of this thesis has been to quantify the role of atmospheric chemistry in climate models by analysing the \ce{SO2} oxidation pathways using the state-of-the-art Earth system model, UKESM1, which features fully interactive atmospheric chemistry and emission-driven aerosol simulations \citep{sellarUKESM1DescriptionEvaluation2019,archibaldDescriptionEvaluationUKCA2020}. UKESM1 provided extensive simulations for AerChemMIP, which has been the primary source of model output for this thesis.

This section details the conclusions of this thesis in the context of the aims and research questions outlined in Section \ref{ch1:sec:questions}.

\section{Future directions}

Despite the known effects of aerosols on atmospheric chemistry \citep[e.g.,][]{oconnorApportionmentPreIndustrial2022}, sulfate aerosol modelling remains an active area of study with many open questions. For example, as a large number of chemical species slows down the progress of the study due to computational resource limits, there remain gaps in treatment of aerosol composition (e.g., nitrate, secondary organic aerosols) in many regions \citep[e.g.,][]{mulcahyDescriptionEvaluationAerosol2020}, and parameterizations of sub-grid processes add to the uncertainty of model prediction so that much of the feedback between aerosol and other components of the Earth system is not yet fully explored \citep{seikiImprovementGlobalCloudSystemResolving2015}. 

Aerosols contribute to significant uncertainty in future warming \citep{watson-parrisLargeUncertaintyFuture2022,wilcoxRegionalAerosolModel2022}

\subsection{Impacts of oxidants on secondary aerosols}

pdClim timeslice experiment from AerChemMIP2 is excellent and would be a great experiment to inform the effects of oxidants. This is a direct follow-up of the results from this thesis. 

There is some continuity here, and I am still the only one looking at aerosol-oxidants in the most detailed way from oxidation straight to ERF, apart from Karset.

\subsection{Looking beyond sulfate aerosol production to other secondary aerosol types and emission sources}

Even though organic soa is not the most important in the historical period. Its importance grows as SO2 emissions decline in the future. Organic soa would also be affected by oxidants and it would be informative to apply the methods presented in this thesis to soa. 

Natural sources of aerosol precursors, such as DMS, are essential in pristine environments, and dedicated simulations targeting DMS oxidation would enhance the understanding of aerosol ERF from natural sources. 



\subsection{piSO2 simulations}
Computational resource constraints aside, histSST-SO2 would be tremendously helpful for understanding the clean climate impact of SO2 emissions and the interaction between oxidant-aerosol-cloud-climate.

\subsection{Region-focused simulations for SO2 and aerosol impacts on climate for historical emissions}

RAMIP focuses on the future impacts of emission location, which is excellent. A similar experimental design would also be helpful in the historical SO2 emissions context.

\subsection{Expanding the analysis to other ESMs}

CMIP7 is on the horizon, and more models than ever will feature online chemistry coupled with radiation. 

Applying the unique, detailed process-level analysis in this thesis as a template for other ESM would help in understanding and diagnosing inter-model behaviour, including Understanding model behaviour with seasonal and regional context  \citep{bellouinRegionalSeasonalRadiative2016}.
